Can ITC be claimed on expenses related to CSR activities?

Verified
As per Section 17(5) of CGST Act, input tax credit is not available for goods or services received by a taxable person for the construction of an immovable property on his own account. However, for other CSR activities, ITC may be available if they are in the course or furtherance of business and not specifically blocked under Section 17(5). The key consideration is whether the expense is for business purposes or purely for CSR compliance.

What is the time limit for issuing a credit note under GST?

Verified
A credit note can be issued before the filing of GSTR-9 for the relevant financial year or before the due date for filing GSTR-9 for that financial year, whichever is earlier. However, it's advisable to issue credit notes as soon as the error is identified to avoid compliance issues. The credit note must be reported in GSTR-1 for the month in which it is issued.

How to handle GST on export of services?

Needs Review
Export of services is treated as zero-rated supply. The exporter can either export services under payment of IGST and claim refund or export under bond/LUT without payment of IGST. The choice depends on cash flow considerations and refund processing time. To claim export of services, the payment must be received in convertible foreign exchange.

What are the compliance requirements for OPC under Companies Act?

Verified
One Person Company (OPC) has relaxed compliance requirements compared to private companies. OPC need not hold Annual General Meeting. However, it must file financial statements and annual return within the prescribed time. The Director of OPC must file a consent in form INC-3 at the time of incorporation. OPC must be converted into private or public company if paid-up share capital exceeds ₹50 lakh or turnover exceeds ₹2 crore.
MCA OPC Compliance

Is TDS applicable on salary paid to NRI employee?

Verified
TDS under Section 192 is applicable on salary paid to NRI employee if the salary is chargeable to tax in India. The key factor is whether the salary is earned in India or for services rendered in India. If the NRI employee is a tax resident of India (stays in India for 182 days or more in the financial year), the entire salary is taxable in India and TDS applies accordingly.
